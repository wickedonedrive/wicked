Professional Working Guidelines

Please read the full guidelines below before proceeding.

Download PDF copy

Clothing

For all departments, when working in the wings dark clothing must be worn. These are ideally to be black and if working within the audience line of sight, full length. There is of course mitigation in warm conditions. All items should be clear of large bright logos. Wicked apparel is permitted to be worn.

For further clarification please speak to Stage Management and your HOD's in order to determine if there is any financial contribution for these requirements.

Footwear

As with clothing, anybody working a wing plot is required to wear dark (ideally black) shoes. Please be mindful of bright stripes & colours (including soles) and ensure these are in keeping with the guidelines. These must be closed toe and heel shoes.

When working near pieces of scenery and transitions (even if crossing up/down stage) it is advised that you wear steel toe cap shoes. These are compulsory for Stage Management, Stage, Automation and LX and discretionary but strongly recommended for Sound, Wigs and Wardrobe.

Please note, if entering the stage area for any reason during rig checks (16:00–18:00) and technical works, Steel Toe Cap shoes are mandatory.

Hair, Jewellery, Watches and Earphones

Small items of jewellery (rings / earrings / watches) are permitted to be worn during the show, however please use common sense when wearing. Avoid anything that may snag or get caught on scenery, wigs or costumes. Please be mindful of smartwatches producing bright light or noise.

Please ensure hair is tied / held back when working near items moving in the wings.

Earphones / Headphones are not permitted to be worn onstage or in the quick change areas. If you require headphones for medical reasons, please consult with company and stage management.

Head Torches

There is no head torch specification however where possible head torches should be used on the lowest setting. When wearing headtorches on the forehead, please be aware of brightness and head movements creating light spill. For advice on suitable head torches, please speak to the LX department or your HOD.

Access / Traffic

Access to stage is for authorised personnel only, in keeping with the technical schedule. When barriers are in use, please do not cross these barriers without permission from whoever is working onstage. When the barriers are in use, the stage is the equivalent of a construction site. This is especially important on maintenance and inspection days.

When beacons are flashing, the stage is a hard hat zone. These can be found in the stairwells stage left and stage right.

Please do not use stage as a cut through or shortcut. Where it is part of your plot, please be aware that stage management (or Technical) may alert you if it is not safe to cross.

Please stick to your plot traffic and timing. This is applicable to both Onstage and Substage.

Crossing downstage of the Oz map is not permitted. Please speak to Stage or Stage Management if you need the map removed for any reason.

Mobile Phones

Mobile Phones are NOT permitted for use onstage. This includes all wings and quick change areas.

Mobile phones can be used in the corridors stage right and lift area stage left, as well as substage. Please ensure that these are set to silent.

Substage Etiquette

Please give priority to anybody carrying baskets or costumes on stairs and in corridors and please be mindful to stick to show timings to limit rushing and panic.

Only sealed containers are to be used substage, especially in quick change areas. This is especially important for tea and coffee around costumes.

Food and Drink

No food and drink is permitted onstage, with the exception of bottled water. All containers must have a lid. Chewing gum is not permitted in any stage, wardrobe or quick change areas.

Noise

Please keep noise levels to a minimum when in the wings. This is especially important when quieter scenes are taking place on stage. Please be as mindful as possible of proximity to wings and the action onstage. This also includes all performers singing offstage as their microphones can often pick up background noise.
